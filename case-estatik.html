Context

Estatik is a professional WordPress plugin that helps real estate agents and agencies build full-featured property websites. It serves customers from single-agent sites to large real estate portals, with 10K+ active installations worldwide.

I was responsible for the full product experience — from research and UX architecture to visual design and design system.

Research & Insights

We conducted market analysis and customer interviews to identify the main pain points. The findings shaped the entire product architecture.

Competitive Analysis

Systematic benchmarking across 7 dimensions identified clear opportunities where Estatik could differentiate from both WordPress plugins and SaaS platforms.

Key findings

  • Registration and login processes were cumbersome — many users abandoned signup
  • Navigation between property lists, templates, and settings was confusing, leading to frequent errors
  • Inconsistent labeling and unclear icons caused misinterpretation of key information
  • Long submission forms with unnecessary fields resulted in incomplete listings
  • Visitors struggled to filter properties by multiple criteria simultaneously

Design Approach

For complex workflows, I created detailed wireframes and flowcharts to align the team early and validate user flows before investing in high-fidelity design.

  • 91 wireframes covering all major user flows
  • Interactive prototypes for usability testing
  • Detailed design specifications for developers, including component behavior, responsive rules, and interaction details
  • A design system based on atomic design principles, ensuring consistent interfaces and speeding up future development

Property Browsing

Two primary browsing modes — grid view for quick scanning and list view with sidebar filters for detailed comparison.

Map View

Interactive map with cluster markers and inline property popups — letting users browse by location without leaving the map view.
